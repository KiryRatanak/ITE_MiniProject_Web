What Others Forget

ខ្មែរ Chronicles was born from a simple conviction: the story of the Khmer people — 2,000 years of empire, resilience, tragedy, and renewal — deserves to be told with the depth and care it warrants.

Our Mission

History is Not the Past.
It is the Present, Remembered.

Cambodia is one of the world's most historically rich nations — and one of the most misunderstood. Too often, Cambodian history in English starts at the Khmer Rouge and ends with Angkor Wat. We reject that reductiveness.

Our mission is to fill in the gaps: the centuries between Angkor and colonialism, the literary traditions that survived occupation, the engineers who built the world's largest pre-industrial city, and the survivors who rebuilt a society from nothing after 1979.

Our Values

What We Stand For

✦

Scholarly Accuracy

Every claim we make is grounded in primary sources, peer-reviewed scholarship, or direct interviews with experts. We cite our work and welcome correction.

❋

Khmer Perspective

We center Cambodian voices and Cambodian scholarship. We resist the framing of Khmer history through Western colonial or Cold War lenses.

◈

Accessible Writing

History shouldn't require a PhD to enjoy. We write for the curious general reader — detailed, context-rich, but always readable and human.

⬡

Cultural Respect

We treat every aspect of Khmer culture — from temple rituals to funeral traditions to cuisine — with the dignity and seriousness they deserve.

⌘

Memory & Justice

The Khmer Rouge genocide must be remembered, documented, and understood. We cover it with care, with testimony, and with a commitment to justice for survivors and victims.

✿

Open Access

All our articles are free. We believe knowledge about Cambodia's history should be accessible to every Cambodian, regardless of their economic circumstance.
