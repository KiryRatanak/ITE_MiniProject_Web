2,000 Years of History

The Khmer
Timeline

Every major turning point in Khmer civilization — from the first riverine kingdoms to the present day — in one scroll.

MAJOR EVENT
EVENT
Era I
Pre-Angkor Period
សម័យមុនអង្គរ — 1st to 8th Century
~100 AD

Funan Kingdom Established

First major state in Southeast Asia emerges at the Mekong delta mouth. Chinese chronicles document its sophisticated trade networks.

~245 AD

Chinese Mission to Funan

Envoys Kang Tai and Zhu Ying visit and record detailed accounts of Funan's society, trade, and governance for Chinese imperial records.

550–600 AD

Rise of Chenla

The Chenla polity absorbs Funan. More distinctly Khmer-speaking rulers begin using Old Khmer in inscriptions alongside Sanskrit.

611 AD

Oldest Khmer Inscription

The stone inscription at Angkor Borei, dated 611 AD, is the oldest known dated text written in the Khmer language — a pivotal moment in linguistic identity.

Era II
The Angkor Empire
សម័យអង្គរ — 802 to 1431
802 AD ★

Jayavarman II Founds the Empire

The devaraja ritual on Phnom Kulen establishes Jayavarman II as the first King of the Khmer Empire, independent from Java. Year 0 of the Angkor era.

889–910 AD

Yasovarman I Builds Yasodharapura

First major urban development at Angkor. Yasovarman I constructs the East Baray — a reservoir 7km × 1.8km — and the hilltop temple Phnom Bakheng.

1113–1150

Suryavarman II & Angkor Wat

Construction of Angkor Wat begins. The largest religious monument ever built — 400 acres, moat 5.5km around, 5 towers representing Mount Meru, dedicated to Vishnu.

1177 AD

Cham Sack of Angkor

The Cham navy sails up the Tonle Sap lake and sacks Angkor in a devastating surprise attack, killing the king and plundering the capital.

1181–1218 ★

Jayavarman VII — The Greatest King

Repels the Cham. Builds Angkor Thom, the Bayon, Ta Prohm, Preah Khan. Establishes 102 hospitals and a road network with 121 rest houses across the empire. Converts to Buddhism.

1296–97

Zhou Daguan Visits Angkor

Chinese diplomat Zhou Daguan spends a year at Angkor and writes "The Customs of Cambodia" — the only surviving eyewitness account of Angkor at its height.

1431 AD

Fall of Angkor

The Thai kingdom of Ayutthaya sacks Angkor. The Khmer court abandons the capital and relocates to Phnom Penh, ending the Angkor era after 629 years.

Era III–IV
Post-Angkor & Colonial Period
1431 – 1953
1594 AD

Sack of Lovek

Thai armies destroy the new Cambodian capital Lovek. Traditional accounts say Cambodia's sacred knowledge was lost forever in this catastrophe.

1863 AD

French Protectorate Established

King Norodom signs treaty with France. Cambodia becomes a French protectorate, ending its independence but saving it from full absorption by Thailand or Vietnam.

1907

Angkor Returned to Cambodia

France negotiates the transfer of Siem Reap and Battambang from Thailand back to Cambodia. Angkor Wat, held by Thailand since 1795, returns to Khmer hands.

NOV 9, 1953 ★

Cambodia Gains Independence

King Norodom Sihanouk achieves full independence from France — peacefully, through political pressure and diplomacy rather than armed uprising. National Day is still celebrated.

Era V–VI
Conflict & Renewal
1953 – Present
1953–1970

Sihanouk's "Golden Age"

Cambodia flourishes under Sihanouk's leadership. Phnom Penh becomes the "Pearl of Asia." Modern universities, hospitals, and a national film industry are established.

APR 17, 1975 ★

Khmer Rouge Seizes Power

Pol Pot's forces enter Phnom Penh. The entire city is evacuated within days. "Year Zero" begins — money is abolished, cities emptied, and Cambodia's darkest chapter opens.

JAN 7, 1979

Liberation of Phnom Penh

Vietnamese forces enter the capital, ending the Khmer Rouge regime. 1.7–2.5 million people had perished in the previous 4 years. The long reconstruction begins.

1991–1993

Paris Peace & UN Elections

The Paris Agreements end the civil war. UNTAC oversees the first free elections. Sihanouk returns as king. Cambodia rejoins the international community.

1992

Angkor Listed as UNESCO World Heritage

UNESCO designates the Angkor Archaeological Park a World Heritage Site, bringing global resources and attention to its restoration and management.

2018

Khmer Rouge Genocide Verdict

The ECCC convicts Nuon Chea and Khieu Samphan of genocide and crimes against humanity — the first legal genocide convictions for the Khmer Rouge atrocities after 30 years of proceedings.

TODAY

Cambodia Rebuilds & Remembers

A young nation with a median age of 25 is rediscovering its ancient roots. Cultural revival, digital heritage, and Khmer language preservation are reshaping national identity in the 21st century.
